Beer Pot

Artist Unknown (Zulu)
(South Africa, Africa), 20th century
About the Object Annotations Related Stories

How is making beer like making babies? For the Zulu, the same ancestral forces that ferment beer also create children in the womb. Zulu families drink beer together to ask their ancestors to help them grow. Round, shiny black pots like this one are at the center of Zulu beer parties. They are designed not only to serve beer to the living but also to help them connect with family ancestors.

1Top View of Beer Pot

You’ve probably noticed that the pot’s patterns are concentrated on the upper half of the vessel, and that it’s best viewed from above. That’s because this pot was meant to sit on the ground – and to forge a direct connection between the drinkers and the ancestors, who reside in the earth.

Process Story: Making Pots

Potters go to great lengths to make their vessels out of clay composed of uniformly small particles. This allows for a more malleable material, smoother surfaces, and stronger pots. Clay harvested from secret locations is allowed to dry. Then it is ground up on a flat grinding stone and sifted with a sieve before water is added.
